Library

The library has evolved far beyond its traditional role as a quiet space filled with books. Today, it serves as a dynamic knowledge hub, blending physical resources with cutting-edge technology to meet the needs of digital-age learners. Modern libraries feature smart catalog systems powered by AI, allowing users to locate materials instantly through mobile apps or interactive kiosks. With 24/7 digital access, students can explore vast collections of e-books, academic journals, and audiobooks from anywhere, eliminating the constraints of physical boundaries. Collaborative workspaces equipped with VR stations, 3D printers, and podcast studios support creative projects and group learning.

Libraries prioritize accessibility, offering assistive technologies like screen readers, Braille displays, and adjustable lighting for visually impaired users. Sustainability is key, with eco-friendly practices such as solar-powered charging stations, paperless transactions, and energy-efficient buildings. AI-powered recommendation systems analyze reading habits to suggest personalized resources, much like streaming platforms. Beyond books, libraries host coding workshops, hackathons, and maker fairs, transforming into innovation labs. They also serve as community centers, providing free Wi-Fi, career counseling, and language-learning programs. With global digital partnerships, students access international archives and research databases, making knowledge inclusive. The modern library is a future-ready learning ecosystem bridging tradition and technology, ensuring equal opportunities for all.
Every bonafide student can obtain Institute Library membership. Students are issued borrower’s cards for the issue and return of books and journals. Borrower’s cards are issued on applying through a prescribed form, recommended by the Head of Department/Department In-charge. The norms for the issuance of books/journals are as follows:
  • Each student can borrow up to 4 books at a time for a period of 14 days.
  • Overdue books incur a fine of Rs. 5 per day per book.
  • Journals and reference materials are for in-library use only and cannot be borrowed.
  • Damaged or lost books must be replaced or compensated at the current market price.
  • Library access is available from 9 AM to 5 PM on weekdays.
